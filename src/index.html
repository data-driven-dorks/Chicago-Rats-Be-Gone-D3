"Rat Capital" - Chicago

In 2018, we saw a total of 37,577 rat complaints in Chicago. Compared with other major US cities like New York and Los Angeles, and cities that were historically troubled by rodent infestations such as Detroit, Chicago not only had the highest total complaint count, but appeared to have the largest number of complaints per capita. Hover over different cities to view the rat complaints per 100,000 population in 2018.

Time Pattern of Rat Complaints

From 2014 to 2018 in Chicago, rat complaints were most frequent in the summer, indicating that rats were potentially most rampant at that time (or at least more likely to be seen). Summer is when temperatures are most moderate for smell of decayed food to spread and for rodents to move around. The higher level of human activities may result in greater amount of trash that appeals to rats. Hover over different circles to view different monthly counts of rat complaints in Chicago.

Surrounding Premises Where Rat Complaints Are Filed

It seems that most complaints were filed near premises or buildings associated with unhandled garbage from 2014 to 2018. Indeed, acting as potential food sources, garbage is most appealing to rodents. It is important for the city government to make sure all properties empty garbage with a timely manner. Hover over each slice of the pie to see the number of rat complaints filed from a certain type of surrounding environment in Chicago from 2014 to 2018.

Vacant Properties May Attract Rats

Rats, see vacant houses as their prime real estate. Such places offer hassle-free shelter; nooks and crannies for hiding and playing; carpeting for nests; and toilet bowls for swimming. Indeed, this plot shows that communities with more vacant properties seem to see more rat complaints in Chicago.
